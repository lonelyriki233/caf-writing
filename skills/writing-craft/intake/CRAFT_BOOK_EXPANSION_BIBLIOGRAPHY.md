# Craft Book Expansion Bibliography

Priority labels:
- P0: greatly improves objective craft judgment.
- P1: valuable after P0.
- P2: genre/specialty expansion.

## P0 — scene mechanics and plot traction
1. Dwight V. Swain — Techniques of the Selling Writer
   - Why: scene/sequel, motivation-reaction units, stimulus-response causality.
   - KB target: `textbooks/scene-mechanics/`.
2. Jack M. Bickham — Scene & Structure
   - Why: scene goal-conflict-disaster, sequel emotion-thought-decision-action.
   - KB target: `textbooks/scene-structure/`.
3. Robert McKee — Story
   - Why: value charge, beats, turning points, progressive complications, climax.
   - KB target: `textbooks/story-mckee/`.
4. John Truby — The Anatomy of Story
   - Why: premise, weakness/need, desire, opponent, moral argument, web of characters.
   - KB target: `textbooks/anatomy-of-story/`.
5. James Scott Bell — Plot & Structure
   - Why: LOCK system, disturbance, doorway, death stakes, plot strengthening.
   - KB target: `textbooks/plot-structure/`.

## P0 — character and emotional engine
6. K.M. Weiland — Creating Character Arcs
   - Why: lie, truth, wound, want/need, positive/flat/negative arcs.
7. Lisa Cron — Wired for Story
   - Why: cognitive reader hooks, protagonist's internal issue, cause-effect meaning.
8. Nancy Kress — Characters, Emotion & Viewpoint
   - Why: viewpoint, emotional credibility, character presentation.

## P0 — prose/revision quality
9. Ursula K. Le Guin — Steering the Craft
   - Why: sentence sound, rhythm, point of view, concrete prose exercises.
10. Renni Browne & Dave King — Self-Editing for Fiction Writers
    - Why: show/tell, dialogue mechanics, interior monologue, proportion, sophistication.
11. Francine Prose — Reading Like a Writer
    - Why: close-reading method for sentences, paragraphs, narration, gesture.
