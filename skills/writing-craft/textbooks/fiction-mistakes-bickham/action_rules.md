# Action Rules

## dont-explain-too-soon
Trigger: opening explains background

Rule: Open with character under pressure; delay explanation until action needs it.

SOP gate: draft gate: no encyclopedia opening.

## dont-make-things-easy
Trigger: plot resolves by convenience

Rule: Increase resistance and cost; avoid easy coincidence solutions.

SOP gate: story_engine gate: obstacle must actively resist.

## dont-stop-story
Trigger: scene pauses for lecture

Rule: Convert explanation into conflict, task, correction, or discovery.

SOP gate: prose_audit gate: exposition must be dramatized.

## dont-use-empty-dialogue
Trigger: dialogue sounds conversational but pointless

Rule: Dialogue must pursue, hide, pressure, refuse, seduce, bargain, or reveal.

SOP gate: dialogue_map gate: action verb per speaker.

## dont-forget-motivation
Trigger: character choice feels arbitrary

Rule: Show motive, pressure, or mistaken belief before major choice.

SOP gate: character_engine gate: choice maps to want/need/wrong strategy.

## dont-overprotect-protagonist
Trigger: hero avoids real loss

Rule: Let choices produce cost, loss, embarrassment, obligation, or danger.

SOP gate: story_engine gate: failure cost required.

## dont-neglect-revision
Trigger: draft treated as final

Rule: Revise by layer: structure, scene, dialogue, prose, not all at once.

SOP gate: revision gate: name changed layer.

## dont-write-static-scenes
Trigger: nothing changes

Rule: Each scene must alter information, relationship, plan, danger, or emotional commitment.

SOP gate: scene_card gate: changed situation required.
