# Action Rules

## sentence-as-unit
Trigger: prose rambles

Rule: Treat each sentence as a unit of perception/thought/action.

SOP gate: prose_audit gate: sentence purpose clear.

## short-sentence-pressure
Trigger: long sentences blur pressure

Rule: Use shorter sentences to reveal attention, turn, or impact, not as gimmick.

SOP gate: draft gate: sentence length tied to pressure.

## remove-filler
Trigger: prose padded

Rule: Cut throat-clearing, generic intensifiers, and pre-explanations.

SOP gate: revision gate: filler removed.

## one-thing-seen
Trigger: description diffuse

Rule: Let a sentence notice one exact thing that matters.

SOP gate: draft gate: concrete perception.

## revision-by-reseeing
Trigger: editing only polishes grammar

Rule: Revision means seeing what each sentence actually does.

SOP gate: revision gate: function audit.

## reader-attention
Trigger: attention unfocused

Rule: Sequence sentences to guide reader attention step by step.

SOP gate: prose_audit gate: attention flow.

## against-formula
Trigger: rules become mechanical

Rule: Use craft rules to sharpen perception, not to produce formulaic prose.

SOP gate: compass gate: rule purpose named.

## sound-and-silence
Trigger: prose overexplains

Rule: Let shortness, silence, and juxtaposition carry meaning when appropriate.

SOP gate: prose_audit gate: avoid overexplaining.
